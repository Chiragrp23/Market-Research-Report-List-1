<p><p>Bearingless encoders are commonly used devices that measure the position, angle, or speed of rotating objects without the need for bearings. The market for bearingless encoders can be broadly categorized into three types. Incremental encoders provide relative measurements, tracking changes in position or speed with pulses. Absolute encoders provide precise absolute measurements, offering a unique code for each position. Other encoders include hybrid or multi-turn encoders that combine the features of both incremental and absolute encoders, providing more comprehensive measurements for complex applications.</p></p>
