<p><p>Fuel antioxidants are chemical additives used to prevent the oxidation process in fuels, thus extending their shelf life and maintaining their quality. Phenolic antioxidants, derived from phenols, are commonly used in fuels due to their effectiveness in inhibiting oxidation. Aminic antioxidants, derived from amines, also play a vital role in preventing oxidative degradation. Phosphite antioxidants, based on organophosphorous compounds, function by decomposing hydroperoxides. Thioester antioxidants, derived from thioesters, are effective at inhibiting fuel degradation caused by oxidation. Lastly, there are other types of fuel antioxidants that may include various compounds with antioxidative properties.</p></p>
