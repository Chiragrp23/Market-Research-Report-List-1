<p><p>Nickel-based superalloys are widely used in industries due to their exceptional high-temperature strength and corrosion resistance. These alloys are categorized into four main types based on their elemental composition: Ni-Cu, Ni-Cr, Ni-Mo, and Others. Ni-Cu alloys contain nickel and copper as primary elements, offering good strength and thermal stability. Ni-Cr alloys possess nickel and chromium, providing resistance to oxidation and creep deformation. Ni-Mo alloys contain nickel and molybdenum, offering superior corrosion resistance. Lastly, the "Others" category includes alloys with various additional elements like cobalt, tungsten, or tantalum, which enhance specific properties based on their applications.</p></p>
