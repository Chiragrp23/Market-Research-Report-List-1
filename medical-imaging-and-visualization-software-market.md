X-rays: This technique uses electromagnetic radiation to capture images of bones and tissues, aiding in the diagnosis of fractures, infections, and lung diseases.</p><p>2. Molecular Imaging: It involves the use of targeted molecular probes to visualize cellular processes, enabling the detection of tumors, diseases, and monitoring of therapy response.</p><p>3. Ultrasound Imaging: This non-invasive technique uses high-frequency sound waves to produce images of organs, blood vessels, and developing fetuses in real-time.</p><p>4. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI): It employs powerful magnets and radio waves to generate detailed images of internal body structures, helping diagnose brain and spinal cord injuries, tumors, and joint abnormalities.</p></p>
