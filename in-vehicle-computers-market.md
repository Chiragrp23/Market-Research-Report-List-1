<p><p>In-vehicle computers, also known as automotive computers or telematics systems, are computing devices specifically designed for use inside vehicles. These computers are integrated with the vehicle's systems and provide various functionalities such as GPS navigation, infotainment, vehicle diagnostics, vehicle data recording, communication, and driver assistance systems. They are primarily used in commercial vehicles, including trucks, buses, and emergency vehicles, but are also becoming increasingly popular in passenger cars.</p><p>The in-vehicle computers market is expected to witness substantial growth in the coming years. The increasing adoption of advanced driver assistance systems and the growing demand for connected cars are major factors driving market growth. Additionally, the rising need for fleet management solutions and the increasing integration of infotainment systems in vehicles are also contributing to the market expansion.</p><p>The market analysis suggests that North America and Europe are the leading regions in terms of market share. This can be attributed to the high penetration of advanced technologies and the strong presence of automobile manufacturers in these regions.
