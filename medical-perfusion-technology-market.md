<p><p>Medical perfusion technology is used to maintain organ function during transplantation by supplying oxygenated blood and nutrients. Hypothermic machine perfusion involves cooling the organ and perfusing it with a cold solution, preserving it to extend its viability. Normothermic machine perfusion involves perfusing the organ with warm blood and oxygen, mimicking natural physiological conditions. Both techniques aim to improve organ quality, reduce organ rejection, and increase transplantation success rates. These technologies are crucial in the field of transplantation medicine as they offer advanced methods for preserving and preparing organs for transplantation.</p></p>
